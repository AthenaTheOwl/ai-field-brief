**Gist:** Weekly data roundup centered on AI's labor and capex footprint: AI cited as the reason for ~40% of US job cuts in May, and a 650x gap in monthly per-employee AI spend between top-1% firms ($7,450) and the typical firm ($11.38).

**Mechanism:** Three concrete numbers landed in the same issue: ~40% of May US job-cut announcements attributed to AI (highest May since 2020); top-1% firms spending $7,450/employee/month on AI vs $11.38 median; Cognition offering up to $10M in credits as a contractual guarantee on agent engineering output.

**Why matters:** The AI spend distribution is power-law, not normal — if you're a TPM benchmarking your org's AI spend against an industry average, you're looking at the wrong number, and vendors are starting to underwrite output with credit guarantees.

**Try:** Add to your weekly metrics: AI spend per employee per month (your number), and the ratio vs $11.38 median and $7,450 top-1%. Also draft a one-page 'agent SLA' template referencing Cognition's $10M-credit guarantee as the reference point for what to negotiate.
